2) Biology - Short Answer In intracellular trafficking, which coat protein complex is primarily responsible for the anterograde transport of vesicles from the endoplasmic reticulum to the Golgi apparatus?

X) S
Y) 3S
Z) 9S

ANSWER: Z) 9S [MR]

BONUS

3) Physics - Multiple Choice Students at Saratoga High School continue studying black holes. They learn that photons can orbit a black hole inside the photon sphere, a region just outside the Schwarzschild radius, where any small push to a photon inward or outward would send a photon either spiraling into the black hole or allowing it to escape the black hole entirely, respectively. What type of equilibrium describes these photon orbits?

W) Asymptotically stable
X) Metastable
Y) Dynamically unstable
Z) Lyapunov [li-a-pu-nov] unstable

ANSWER: Y) DYNAMICALLY UNSTABLE [MR]

TOSS-UP

4) Math - Multiple Choice Lawson the ant is placed on the bottom left square in a 3x3 square grid. Every second, he randomly chooses to move to a vertically or horizontally adjacent square, with an equal probability to move to each adjacent square. What is the probability that Lawson is in the center square after 2026 seconds have passed?

W) 1/9
X) 1/6
Y) 1/3
Z) 1/2

ANSWER: Y) 1/3 [QG]

BONUS

4) Math - Short Answer Lawson the ant decides that a 3x3 grid is too small, so he renovates the environment to be a 5x5 grid instead. He starts in the center square and follows the same procedure as before. Each second, he moves to an adjacent square, with an equal probability to move to each adjacent square. What is the probability that Lawson is back in the center after 4 seconds?

ANSWER: 7/48 [QG]

TOSS-UP

5) Earth and Space - Multiple Choice Historically, Vega has been used as the zero-point reference star for the magnitude scale, meaning it was assigned apparent magnitude 0 at all wavelengths and every star's magnitude was measured relative to Vega's. However, more modern research indicates that Vega might not be the ideal calibration star it was once thought of. Which of the following is NOT a reason for why Vega is an unreliable calibration star?

W) Its high recessional velocity causes significant Doppler shifts, altering its magnitude inconsistently across wavelengths
X) It has been discovered to be a variable star, making its brightness inconsistent
Y) Its rapid rotation causes temperature differences, causing it to have variable emission spectra across its surface
Z) It has unique infrared excesses due to a debris disk, altering its spectrum

ANSWER: W) ITS HIGH RECESSIONAL VELOCITY CAUSES SIGNIFICANT DOPPLER SHIFTS, ALTERING ITS MAGNITUDE INCONSISTENTLY ACROSS WAVELENGTHS [MR]

BONUS

5) Earth and Space - Multiple Choice Which of the following does the Torino scale measure?

W) Near-Earth object impacts
X) Subsurface volcanic eruptions
Y) Impacts of CFCs in the atmosphere
Z) Size of sunspots

ANSWER: W) NEAR-EARTH OBJECT IMPACTS [AG]

TOSS-UP

6) Y-Risk - Multiple Choice Students at Saratoga High School are researching a very important question: What does the fox say? Max thinks he can solve for what the fox says, if only he had a theory of everything. Which of the following is not a candidate for a theory of everything?

W) Pilot Wave Theory
X) Quantum Loop Gravity
Y) String Theory
Z) Casual Dynamical Triangulations ANSWER: W) PILOT WAVE THEORY [MR]
BONUS

6) Y-Risk - Short Answer Which of the following mechanisms allows models such as ResNet to take advantage of much deeper neural network architectures?

W) Feature extraction pyramids
X) Attention modules
Y) Feature map caching
Z) Skip connections

ANSWER: Z) SKIP CONNECTIONS [WC]
